ABOUT US

Who we are

Sherril Donaldson, Mikey Lee aka DJ Mikey D and Clive Tennant

ACT was formed in early 2017, by Michael Lee, Sherril Donaldson & Clive Tennant, as a means of consolidating two other community enterprises operated by one or more of the named individuals. The creation of ACT was a step to ensure the sustainability of the both enterprises and the creation of other services for our community.

The first of the two enterprises was The Family Sports Fun Day. First started in 2011, with just over 100 people in attendance, this event was started because of the need to bring our Caribbean families together and also to fill a gap created by the demise of the funded “Caribbean Carnival".

Since its inception The Family Sports Fun Day has enjoyed the gradual increase of participation culminating last year, 2018, with a record attendance of over 1000 people turning up to each of the events.

The events are scheduled to take place on the 1st Sunday in June, July and August at The Xcel Leisure Centre, Canley, Coventry.

The second enterprise, The ArawaK Radio Station, started in March 2015 at the Broad Street Young People’s Centre, Foleshill, Coventry. It was started because of a lack of diversity in radio broadcasting for the Jamaican & Caribbean community in the city of Coventry. It was also an opportunity for young people with Caribbean heritage to enhance their desires to learn about radio broadcasting, sound engineering and to host radio shows with the music they do not hear, as the norm, on mainstream radio.

The station is run entirely by volunteers and currently there are over 30 presenters offering a 24/7 services to our listeners. The radio also offers and allows local BME businesses to advertise their products, services and events to a very specific, largely BME, audience.

Through the development of ACT in 2019 there will be significant growth for both the Family Fun Day and the radio.
